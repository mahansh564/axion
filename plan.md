# 🧠 Project: Axion

## Overview

**Axion** is an AI system that autonomously conducts research, stores knowledge, ingests personal experiences, and evolves beliefs over time.

Unlike traditional RAG or note-taking systems, this project combines:

* Autonomous research agents
* Structured long-term memory
* Personal experience ingestion
* Temporal knowledge graphs
* Belief synthesis and evolution

The goal is to move from:

> “AI that retrieves information”
> to
> “AI that builds understanding over time”

---

# 🎯 Core Thesis

A true second brain must integrate:

* **Information (objective, external)**
* **Experience (subjective, internal)**

And transform them into:

* **Beliefs**
* **Intuition**
* **Evolving understanding**

---

# 🧱 System Architecture

## 1. Research Layer

### Purpose

Autonomously gather and synthesize knowledge.

### Features

* Task-based overnight research runs
* Query decomposition into sub-questions
* Multi-agent parallel exploration
* Source collection (web, papers, etc.)

### Output

* Facts
* Claims
* Summaries
* Source links
* Confidence scores

---

## 2. Experience Layer (Key Differentiator)

### Purpose

Capture personal, contextual, and subjective data.

### Input Channels

#### 🎙️ Voice Notes

* Raw thoughts, reflections, reactions
* Transcribed and processed

Extract:

* Entities
* Emotions
* Uncertainty
* Personal stance

---

#### 💬 Conversations

* AI interactions
* Human discussions (manually logged)

Capture:

* Questions asked
* Disagreements
* Follow-up curiosity

---

#### 📚 Highlights & Annotations

* Books, PDFs, articles

Signal:

> “This mattered to me”

---

#### 🧍 Social Inputs

* Conversations with trusted people
* Source credibility weighting

---

#### 🧠 Daily Reflections

Prompts:

* What surprised me?
* What confused me?
* What do I want to explore?

---

### Output

* Experience nodes
* Emotional tags
* Personal stance signals

---

## 3. Memory Layer

A hybrid system combining multiple storage paradigms.

---

### 📄 Document Memory

* Structured long-form documents
* Section-aware retrieval

---

### 🕸️ Knowledge Graph (Core)

Stores:

* Entities
* Relationships
* Temporal changes

Supports:

* Contradictions
* Provenance tracking
* Belief evolution

---

### 🧠 Episodic Memory

Logs:

* What was researched
* When
* What conclusions were formed

---

## 4. Synthesis Layer (Most Important)

### Purpose

Transform raw data into **beliefs and understanding**

---

### Inputs

* Research findings
* Experience data

---

### Outputs

#### 🧠 Belief Nodes

* “User believes X”
* Confidence score
* Timestamp

---

#### 🔄 Evolving Views

* Track how beliefs change over time
* Preserve old beliefs (no deletion)

---

#### ❓ Open Questions

* What remains uncertain
* What needs further research

---

### Example

**Research:**

* “mTOR extends lifespan in mice”

**User Experience:**

* “Feels overhyped”

**Synthesis:**

* “User is skeptical about mTOR relevance to humans”

---

## 5. Retrieval Layer (Q&A Interface)

### Purpose

Query the system like a conversational brain

---

### Output Format

When asked:

> “What do I think about X?”

Return:

* Research consensus
* Personal experiences
* Beliefs
* Confidence levels
* Contradictions
* Evolution over time

# ⚠️ Critical Risks

## 1. Complexity Overload

* Too many components at once

**Mitigation:**

* Start simple (voice + graph)

---

## 2. Hallucinations

* Incorrect knowledge stored as truth

**Mitigation:**

* Provenance tracking
* Confidence scoring
* Evaluation framework

---

## 3. Stale Knowledge

* Information becomes outdated

**Mitigation:**

* Temporal tracking
* Expiration logic
* Contradiction detection

---

## 4. Over-Automation

* Replacing thinking instead of augmenting it

**Mitigation:**

* Keep human-in-the-loop
* Emphasize reflection
